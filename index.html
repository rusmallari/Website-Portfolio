Background

About Me

In 2002, my family moved from Masantol, Pampanga to California — adjusting to life in a small two-bedroom apartment. As a first-generation college student, I navigated a new culture, an unfamiliar education system, and the pressure to succeed.

While many expected me to pursue medicine, I chose Computer Science. What fascinated me most was how fast computers learn and adapt — and that curiosity hasn't stopped since. I chose a path where I could build things that actually solve problems.

Balancing academics with supporting my family taught me resilience. I learned that real growth comes from embracing challenges, not avoiding them. That mindset shows up in everything I build.

Outside of code I stay balanced through the gym and hiking — both keep me sharp and grounded.
